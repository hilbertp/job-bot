## My Approach and what I focus on

A product manager’s job is to make sure the team is solving a real customer problem in a way that works for the customer, can be built, and makes sense for the business. That means deeply understanding customers, the data, the business context, and the technology, then leading discovery to identify the biggest risks before delivery. The PM does not define success as shipping features, but as enabling the product team to deliver outcomes customers love and the business needs.

The biggest problems and risk should be tackled head-on first. The most important categories are the value risk (will customers actually buy it?), usability risk (will they understand it quickly?), feasibility risk (can we build the system quickly and cheaply enough?) and business viability risk (can our business handle the problems around the product, legal, sales, support etc?)

As a PM, my role is not to dictate solutions but to identify problems and increase the chances to solve them by structuring team communication, keeping them aligned across engineering, operations and support (full DevOps chain) and ensuring decisions/assumptions can be audited, revised and reversed.
